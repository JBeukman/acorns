Our curriculum

At Acorns Group we use the Early Years Foundation Stage (EYFS) to support childrenâ€™s development and provide the foundations to our curriculum. We understand that children develop quickly in the early years, and our Early Years Practitioners do all they can to help children have the best possible start in life.
We offer provision which enables each child to develop their personalities, talents and abilities irrespective of ethnicity, culture or religion, home language, family background, learning difficulties, disabilities or gender.

Maths

Maths is supported throughout our provision at Acorns and designated areas have been created in our Tweenies and Preschool room. Practitioners support children to work towards being able to count reliably, become confident problem solvers and have a strong knowledge of shapes, space and measures. Practitioners recognise that it is vital for children to be engaged, motivated and be able to think critically for themselves to become confident in all areas of maths development.

Science

We recognise that young children are naturally inquisitive, full of questions about the world around them and have the drive to investigate how things work. During planned science sessions children take part in simple experiments to help them begin to make sense of the world around them. As well as working through planned science activities children are provided daily opportunities for scientific discovery, enabling them to channel their curiosity, and foster their investigative skills

Through our planned English sessions and continuous provision children acquire the pre-reading and handwriting skills they need to become confident readers and writers.

P.E.

P.E sessions have been developed throughout the setting for children to take part in on a daily basis as well as continuous provision to develop childrenâ€™s physical education. These sessions help children to develop their motor skills, improve their balance and coordination and provide holistic benefits for their mobility. Children benefit from the growth of strong muscles and bones through physical development and it also improves mental health at the same time. Through trial and error children develop a way to deal with various challenges in their lives, helping with confidence, self-esteem and resilience.

Music

Children take part in planned music sessions. These sessions include the use of musical instruments, body percussion, and singing. This looks at aspects of musical learning and development which are hearing and listening, vocalising and singing. Music ignites all areas of child development, from birth where music can be used to calm and sooth children, up to children becoming school ready where the sessions can support social skills.

Acorns Group Day Nursery was established in January 2011 and is located in a modern purpose built two storey building in the heart of the village of Eldwick. The location makes it central and convenient for parents in the village and surrounding area. The Nursery is located in a modern complex with also includes a NHS Health Centre, Chemist and Dental practice. The Nursery is in close proximity to the local Primary school which is the feeder school for our children.
